Memories fade with time. They become blurry distorted even warped. But what if we could recall a memory? What if we could record a forgotten thought and archive it for future reference? Peter a home inventor has cracked the code to memory recall and invented a device that coalesces lost memories into crystal clear focus. But when distant memoirs stir up pain and regret Peter seeks out greener pastures challenging the laws of neurophysics. What ensues is a journey caught somewhere between yesterday and tomorrow. On this fateful day Peter will discover the difference between a life planned and a life lived with his fate caught somewhere in the middle.
