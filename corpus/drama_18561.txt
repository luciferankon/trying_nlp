A Venezuelan single mother (Elizabeth Mendoza) moves to Minnesota to pursue a nursing career. She falls in love and marries a high school gym teacher (Robert Larson). Once she becomes an US citizen she sponsors her son's legality in the United Estates with the objective of providing him a better life. Her son (Alejandro Marquez Mendoza) moves with his mother and her husband and starts his senior year in high school where he faces different cultural standards and social challenges. The Larsons stand firm through shaky times. The story tells about some problems that immigrants face when they move to America and the importance of family support.
