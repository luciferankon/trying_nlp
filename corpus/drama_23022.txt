The remarkable story of a heroic woman Noor Inayat Khan codename "Madeleine." Trained by Britain's Special Operations Executive she was the first female wireless operator to be flown into occupied France during WWII being infiltrated into the Paris area where within days of her arrival almost her entire circuit was arrested by the Gestapo making 'poste-Madeleine' the last radio link between France and England. The story details Noor's life from her birth in 1914 in Moscow to an American mother and an Indian Muslim father who founded the first Sufi Order in the West through to her death at the hands of the Gestapo in 1944.
