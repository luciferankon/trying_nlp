The colorful and culturally-significant Jankunu dance tradition of the Garifuna people of Belize is presented. The Garifuna are a Central American people of West African and Native American descent. In addition to discussions of the dance and music styles involved the Jankunu raises valuable issues of syncretism colonial history performance and embodiment of culture and experience and cultural survival.
