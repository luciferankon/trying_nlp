The Maclean brothers Paul and Norman live a relatively idyllic life in rural Montana spending much of their time fly fishing. The sons of a minister the boys eventually part company when Norman moves east to attend college leaving his rebellious brother to find trouble back home. When Norman finally returns the siblings resume their fishing outings and assess both where they've been and where they're going. The true story about two boys growing up in rural Montana while rebelling against their stern minister father. Eldest brother Norman eventually leaves home and becomes a disciplined grounded professor while younger brother Paul becomes a rebellious journalist and descends into gambling and liquor. Their mornings are spent in school and religious study while their afternoons are devoted to fly fishing for trout in the Blackfoot River.
