On Easter 2015 the year a million refugees arrived in Germany arsonists attacked a refugee block in a quiet village in the state of Saxony-Anhalt. Overnight Troeglitz's burnt-out roof became the international icon of a new wave of xenophobic violence in the country. For 17 months up to the state election in March 2016 and its aftermath the Canadian director discovered the "silent majority" of the people who live there and their own century-old migrant culture. Poetic moving humorous and critical.
