A Spy for Peace chronicles the life and work of humanitarian philanthropist and business leader Leon H. Charney. From humble beginnings in Bayonne New Jersey Charney grew into a man who earned a JD from Brooklyn Law School became a successful entrepreneur by his mid-20's and at 40 was an instrumental player in the historic 1978 Camp David Accords signed by Egyptian President Anwar Sadat and Israeli Prime Minister Menachem Begin. Today Charney owns and manages real estate holdings valued at $1.2 billion. A man of faith with an unwavering devotion to peace Charney has bestowed gifts on numerous charities and endowed many institutions and has traveled the world as a cantorial singer. In A Spy for Peace colleagues friends and loved ones paint an intimate portrait of Mr. Charney's life and work.
