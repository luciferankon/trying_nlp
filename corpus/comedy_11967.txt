The Bumsteads receive two letters: one announcing a high-school reunion dinner the other a "course" showing how to pick winners at the race track. Later when Dagwood calls to tell Blondie he has received a two-dollar-and-fifty-cent weekly raise friends present at their home to discuss the reunion dinner with Blondie jump to the conclusion that the raise was $250 weekly. and expect the Bumsteads to pay for the dinner. Dagwood gets fired again and horse-racing a race-track touts bookies and assorted characters also get involved.
