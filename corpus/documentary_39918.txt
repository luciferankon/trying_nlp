For immigrant Polish families along the shores of Lake Erie Mardi Gras was known by another name: Paczki Day in honor of their fried pre-Lenten yeast donuts called paczki ('poonsh-kee'). Thanks to baker Tim Kiedrowski the tradition is still alive and it's Paczki Time again in Lorain County Ohio a three-day celebration of family friends food and fun. This close-knit small-town multi-generational Polish culture is the true heart of America. It may be harder to find but it's not lost.
