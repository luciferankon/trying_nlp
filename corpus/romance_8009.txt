A writer/producer is about to make a big career jump and has a lot of activity going on in his life. But emotionally he's dead. Through a fantastical event he meets someone. They fall in love. She wants to defy logic and hop on the passing train of time with him. He lets his mind rationalize and hesitates. She is gone and he finds himself in a desperate race to get her back. Will the magic that brought them together be strong enough to reunite them or is it too long? If you believe in something strong enough and hold on to it before time passes it can be real. Black Racklin has everything in life to live for but he's dead... emotionally that is. He has dreams of a passing train representing "time" passing by with an image of himself hesitating to hop on it. Through a fantastical occurrence Katherine comes into Black's life and he starts to feel alive. They fall in love. Katherine experiences the same real magic. She follows her heart willing to seize the moment and defy the natural logic of things. Ready to hop on the passing train of time with him. Black lets his logic prevail and pauses then decides to hop on with her. The pause was too long. Time doesn't wait. Black loses Katherine and tries to get her back. But is it too late?
