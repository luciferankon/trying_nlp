Court métrage réalisé ŕ partir d'un počme de Michčle Lalonde créé en 1970 ŕ l'occasion de de la premičre Nuit de la poésie ŕ Montréal. Le texte se déroule sur un montage de photos chocs appuyées d'une trame sonore suggestive dans un film dénonçant l'impérialisme économique et culturel des classes dominantes. Speak White is a French language poem composed by Québécois writer Michčle Lalonde in 1968. It was first recited in 1970 and was published in 1974 by Editions de l'Hexagone Montreal. It denounced the poor situation of French-speakers in Quebec and takes the tone of a collective complaint against English-speaking Quebecers. Her poem is not however directed solely or even primarily at English Canada often citing British and American references such as Shakespeare Keats the Thames the Potomac and Wall Street as its symbols of linguistic oppression.
