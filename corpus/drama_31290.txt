A single mother is prescribed a controversial anti-depressant called Distral and quickly becomes dependant but discovers that giving up the drug causes terrible side-effects. Unable to find support for her plight she mounts a personal crusade against the pharmaceutical company who produce the pills. Meanwhile a cynical marketing executive for the company has been trying to suppress all bad publicity and promote Distral as a wonder-product but as he watches his sister suffer from terminal cancer he begins to question his own ethics.
