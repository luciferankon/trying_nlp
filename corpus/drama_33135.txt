Siddalee a famous New York playwright is quoted in Time magazine and infuriates her dramatic Southern mother. A long-distant fight wages until her mother's friends (and members of the Yaya Sisterhood) kidnap Siddalee and take her "home" to the South where they hope to explain her mother's history and to patch up the rift between mother and daughter. Playwright Siddalee Walker on the verge of opening a new play gives a magazine interview in which she discusses her unhappy childhood. Her mother is furious and cuts her off. Her mother's friends who formed a secret society the Ya-Ya Sisterhood as children kidnap Siddalee from her New York apartment and take her home to Louisiana where they explain why her mother mistreated her with help from the secret Ya-Ya scrapbook (and copious flashbacks).
