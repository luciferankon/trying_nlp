A hundred years ago - Nov. 5 1913 - 40000 people gathered in Sylmar to watch the water arrive for the first time via the Los Angeles Aqueduct from the Owens Valley. It took 5000 workers five years to complete the $23-million project which was excavated with dynamite hand shovels and mule power in rocky canyons and searing desert expanses.
