A 9-year-old American Jewish boy David meets a poor 9-year-old street urchin of Arab descent Kamal who sells postcards to tourists in the stone-enclosed Old City of Jerusalem. At first glance Kamal seems bright and energetic but in reality he's suffering under the tyrannical presence of his grandfather who oversees the living of his family which includes Kamal's mother and two sisters. Nine-year-old David a well-off American Jew comes to visit his estranged father in Jerusalem to check the authenticity of an expensive coin for his collection. Nine-year-old Arab street urchin Kamal sees David's money and looks upon him as the cash cow needed to remove himself from his abused and preyed upon circumstances. Theft and extortion turn into a friendship bond between these boys as they find themselves targeted by a street gang and local authorities.
