A house that is dismantled bears a fascination as process of revealing different layers of history of the building which is destined to eventually disappear. The finalized product a new house appears as cohesive static masterpiece that the architect is congratulated for. Technical practices uses of tools and team work are disguised by the sight of a new building. It stands as a metaphor for an anthropologist's finished article book photography or film. Like for the house the technical aspects of anthropologists' works are often hidden practices at home or in libraries and remain unidentifiable in the final product. Scholars are acknowledged for the prestigious architectural white-collar facets of their work while the technical blue collar facets associated with construction sites remain mystified.
