Imagine discovering you are not the person you thought you were. That you have a family a history an ethnicity you never knew. How would this discovery impact your life the lives of those around you your vision of yourself and society? Michael Fosberg's astonishing true-life journey to search for his biological father was first presented on stage in Chicago in October of 2001 and has since toured to venues across the country - theaters performing arts centers festivals colleges high schools at conferences for corporations and community groups - engaging audiences of all ages genders and colors in deeply meaningful dialogue after each show. The show has been hailed by critics and educators as "positively cathartic" in its ability to allow audience members to question their own perceptions about race identity stereotypes.
