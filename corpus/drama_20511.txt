'Hit The North' is a lyrical and poetic road movie which follows a Hitcher up the A1 motorway in search of his father during the UK Miner's strike of the 1980s. Characters from the road's past haunt the Hitcher's journey. Along the way he encounters a philosopher an ex-soldier a mystical monk a lover; with whom he discusses feminism war and the vagaries of religion. Past present and future meld we see the Hitcher as a child and man defined by the road. Eventually he discovers his father in Glasgow but the Hitcher is unable to connect to a personal or political history. He wanders in search of a past of lost possibilities of love as child witnesses look on in defiance of time.
