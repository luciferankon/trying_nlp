In Campina Grande Northeastern of Brazil the three fifty and something year old illiterate Barbosa sisters (Maria das Neves a.k.a. "Maroca" Regina a.k.a."Poroca" and Conceiçăo a.k.a. "Indaiá") are daughters of cousins and born blind. They have survived playing "ganzá" and begging on the streets of Campina Grande since their childhood. The director Roberto Berliner discloses in this remarkable documentary their sad stories with poverty rape abuse inclusive sexual and love but never corny; on the contrary with a great message of strength faith and hope.
