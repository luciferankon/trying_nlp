Between good and evil quietly pulses a yawning fathom known as genius. It is the fuel which propels both the artist and in some instances the murderer. John Wilkes Booth was both. Or rather he became both. The genius which eluded him as an artist he discovered as a killer. Assassinating Lincoln the freer of slaves delivered to John the epiphany of genius he otherwise would have never known. Spanning a period of 40 years Booth is the definitive account of the life of John Wilkes Booth which ultimately attrited to Abraham Lincoln's murder and death.
