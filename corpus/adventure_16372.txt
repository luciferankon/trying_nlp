The series sees him venture to Argentina where he works as a gaucho and learns how to ride a horse and herd 500-strong cattle across rivers and canyons. Risking life and limb he treks 400 kilometers with the Tuareg salt caravans across the Azalai highways of the Sahara desert. Next up he heads to Northern Luzon where he learns the spiritual importance of the rice trade to the Ifuago people as he trains as a rice farmer. In India's bustling Kolkata his endurance is truly tested as he works tirelessly as a rickshaw puller. And finally Jim explores the incredible bond between man and elephant as he becomes a mahout. Living amongst the Karen people of Asia he is taught how to steer bath and feed his elephant all in the confines of the intense jungle.
